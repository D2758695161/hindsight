# Marketing Post Writing Guidelines

This guide outlines the essential rules for drafting marketing posts to ensure consistency, clarity, and effectiveness when communicating with our target audience.

## Target Audience
Our primary audience is **technical leaders**, including CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and staff-plus engineers. Content should be tailored to their expertise, maintaining a high level of technical detail without simplification.

## Style & Tone
*   **Conversational and Human:** Posts should sound like they're written by a smart human, not a formal press release.
*   **Confident and Direct:** Maintain a confident and direct tone.
*   **Avoid Corporate Jargon:** Steer clear of corporate speak, buzzwords, and overly enthusiastic terms.

## Voice
*   **Team-Oriented:** All marketing posts must be signed as "the team."
*   **Impersonal Pronouns:** Never use "I" or "we."

## Structure
*   **Concise Paragraphs:** For platforms like LinkedIn, limit posts to a maximum of 4 short paragraphs.
*   **Character Limit:** LinkedIn posts should not exceed 600 characters.
*   **Engaging Introduction:** Begin by highlighting the user benefit or the importance of the topic, rather than a direct announcement. Avoid long-winded introductions.
*   **Call to Engagement:** Conclude every post with a single open-ended question to encourage reader interaction.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
*   Target technical leaders.
*   Maintain a high level of technical detail.
*   Lead with user benefits or topic importance.
*   End with a single open-ended question.
*   Sign posts as "the team."

### Don't
*   Simplify content for the audience.
*   Use corporate jargon or buzzwords.
*   Use terms like "thrilled," "excited," "delighted," "game-changer," "revolutionary," "leverage," or "synergy."
*   Include emojis.
*   Use hashtags.
*   Begin with long introductions or direct announcements.
*   Use "I" or "we."
