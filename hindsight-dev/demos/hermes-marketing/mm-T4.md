The following guidelines should be followed when drafting marketing posts:

### **Target Audience**
*   Technical leaders, including CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and staff-plus engineers.
*   Do not simplify content; maintain a high level of technical detail.

### **Writing Style & Tone**
*   **Voice:** Sound like a smart human, conversational, not a formal press release.
*   **Tone:** Confident and direct.
*   **Language:** Avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords, and overly enthusiastic terms.

### **Structure**
*   **Introduction:** Avoid long-winded introductions. Start by highlighting the user benefit or the importance of the topic, rather than a direct announcement.
*   **Paragraphs:** For LinkedIn posts, use a maximum of 4 short paragraphs.
*   **Length:** LinkedIn posts should have a maximum of 600 characters.
*   **New Announcements:** For new product or feature announcements, include a concrete metric or outcome in the second sentence. Avoid vague benefits.
*   **Element Order:** The correct order for elements is: Body, followed by the Signature, and then the Closing Question.
*   **Signature:** Marketing posts must be signed off as "— the team" on its own line at the very end, preceded by a blank line.
*   **Closing:** Conclude with a single open-ended question to encourage engagement. This question must appear on its own line after the signature.

### **Do's and Don'ts**

**Do:**
*   Focus on user benefits and topic importance.
*   Maintain a confident and direct tone.
*   Include concrete metrics/outcomes for announcements.
*   End with an open-ended question for engagement.
*   Sign off as "— the team".

**Don't:**
*   Use corporate jargon or buzzwords (e.g., "thrilled", "excited", "delighted", "game-changer", "revolutionary", "leverage", "synergy").
*   Use "I" or "we" in the posts.
*   Include emojis.
*   Use hashtags.
*   Write long-winded introductions.
*   Simplify content for the technical audience.
*   Use vague benefits in announcements.
