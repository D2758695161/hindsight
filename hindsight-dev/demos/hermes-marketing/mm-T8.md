Here are the current writing style, tone, structure, voice, and do/don't rules to follow when drafting marketing posts:

### Overall Tone and Voice

*   **Confident and Direct:** Maintain a tone that is assured and straightforward.
*   **Conversational and Human:** Posts should sound like they're written by a smart human, not a formal press release.
*   **Target Audience:** Write for technical leaders, including CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and staff-plus engineers.
*   **Technical Detail:** Do not simplify content; maintain a high level of technical detail.
*   **Avoid Corporate Jargon:** Steer clear of corporate buzzwords and jargon.

### Writing Style and Language

*   **Numbers:** Always use digits for numbers; do not spell them out.
*   **Community Recognition:** Instead of explicitly thanking people, name specific community actions (e.g., "broke it," "improved it," "opened PRs," "filed issues").
*   **Speaker Names:** When listing two or more speakers, use their actual names and titles when known, rather than role-only labels (e.g., "our VP of Product").

### Structure and Formatting

*   **Introductions:** Avoid long-winded introductions.
*   **Opening:** Start posts by highlighting the user benefit or the importance of the topic, rather than with a direct announcement.
*   **LinkedIn Posts:**
    *   Limit to a maximum of 600 characters.
    *   Structure with a maximum of 4 short paragraphs.
*   **Milestone Posts (Stars, Signups, Downloads, ARR, Customers):**
    *   The first line must lead with a provocative claim about what the milestone means for the audience, without mentioning the metric.
    *   The metric should be placed in the second sentence as proof of this claim.
*   **New Product/Feature Announcements:**
    *   Include a concrete metric or outcome in the second sentence.
    *   Avoid vague benefits.
*   **Webinar/Event Posts:**
    *   Place the date and time on its own line, separate from the body.
    *   Use the format: `Tuesday Apr 14, 2pm ET`.
*   **Order of Elements:** The correct order for elements in marketing posts is: Body, followed by the signature, and then the closing question.
*   **Signature:** Sign off as "— the team" on its own line at the very end, preceded by a blank line.
*   **Closing Question:** Posts must conclude with a single open-ended question to encourage engagement, appearing on its own line after the signature.

### Do's and Don'ts

*   **Do Use:**
    *   Confident and direct language.
    *   Conversational and human tone.
    *   High technical detail for the target audience.
    *   Digits for all numbers.
    *   Specific verbs like "identify," "reduce," "cap," or "limit" instead of vague terms.
*   **Don't Use:**
    *   Corporate jargon or buzzwords.
    *   Words like "just," "thrilled," "excited," "delighted," "game-changer," "revolutionary," "leverage," "synergy," "tighten," or "spot."
    *   "I" or "we" in the post body (use "— the team" for the signature).
    *   Explicit thanks; instead, describe community actions.
    *   Emojis.
    *   Hashtags.
    *   Long-winded introductions.
    *   Vague benefits in announcements.
