Here is a comprehensive guide for drafting marketing posts across LinkedIn, X, blog teasers, newsletters, and announcements, encompassing style, tone, structure, voice, and do/don't rules.

### Target Audience
The primary audience for marketing posts consists of technical leaders, including CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and staff-plus engineers.

### General Style, Tone, and Voice Guidelines

*   **Tone:** Confident and direct.
*   **Voice:** Maintain a professional and informative voice, focusing on user benefits and concrete outcomes. Avoid informal language and overly enthusiastic expressions.
*   **Pronouns:** Do not use first-person pronouns ("I" or "we").
*   **Buzzwords:** Avoid corporate buzzwords.
*   **Specific Language:** When acknowledging community contributions, instead of explicitly thanking people, describe what the community did (e.g., broke it, improved it, opened PRs, filed issues). For specific actions, prefer verbs like 'identify', 'reduce', 'cap', or 'limit' over vague terms like 'tighten' or 'spot'.
*   **Numbers:** All numbers must be written using digits, not spelled out.

### General Structure

*   **Lead:** Start by leading with the user benefit or explaining why the content matters to the audience.
*   **Signature:** The signature must be on its own line at the very end, formatted as "— the team", with a blank line preceding it.
*   **Closing:** Always end with a single open-ended question, which must come on its own line after the signature.

### Channel and Post Type Specific Rules

#### LinkedIn Posts
*   **Length:** Maximum of 600 characters.
*   **Paragraphs:** Contain a maximum of 4 short paragraphs.

#### New Product or Feature Announcements
*   **Metric/Outcome:** A concrete metric or outcome must be included in the second sentence.

#### Milestone Marketing Posts (e.g., stars, signups, downloads, ARR, customers)
*   **First Line:** Must lead with a provocative claim about what the milestone means for the audience (do not mention the metric here).
*   **Second Sentence:** The metric should be mentioned in the second sentence.

#### Webinar/Event Marketing Posts
*   **Date and Time:** Place on its own line in the format 'Tuesday Apr 14, 2pm ET' for scannability.
*   **Speakers:** When listing two or more speakers, use their actual names and titles. If names are unknown, ask the user.

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
*   **Be Confident and Direct:** Use a tone that conveys authority and clarity.
*   **Focus on User Benefits:** Lead your posts with how the content benefits the user or why it's important to them.
*   **Use Specific Language:** Describe community actions concretely and use precise verbs instead of vague terms.
*   **Include Metrics:** For announcements and milestones, provide concrete metrics or outcomes.
*   **End with an Open-Ended Question:** Always encourage engagement with a thoughtful question at the end.
*   **Format Dates/Times Clearly:** Use 'Tuesday Apr 14, 2pm ET' for events.
*   **Use Digits for Numbers:** Always write numbers using digits (e.g., 5 instead of five).
*   **Sign Off as "— the team":** Ensure proper formatting with a blank line before the signature.

**Don't:**
*   **Use First-Person Pronouns:** Avoid "I" or "we."
*   **Include Emojis:** Emojis are not permitted in marketing posts.
*   **Use Hashtags:** Never use hashtags.
*   **Use Corporate Buzzwords:** Steer clear of jargon and corporate speak.
*   **Use Banned Words:** Avoid the words: `thrilled`, `excited`, `delighted`, `game-changer`, `revolutionary`, `leverage`, `synergy`, and `just`.
*   **Explicitly Thank People:** Instead, describe their contributions without direct thanks.
*   **Use Vague Verbs:** Avoid 'tighten' or 'spot'; opt for 'identify', 'reduce', 'cap', or 'limit'.
*   **Exceed LinkedIn Limits:** Keep LinkedIn posts to a maximum of 600 characters and 4 short paragraphs.
*   **Start Milestone Posts with Metrics:** Lead with a provocative claim first, then the metric.
